## Module 3: Profile Optimization

### Video 3.1: Business Name & Category (5 minutes)

**Thumbnail:** "Name & Category" with settings icon

**Script:**

"Now that you're verified, it's time to optimize. And we start with the most important fields: your business name and category.

Let's start with business name.

Your business name seems simple, right? Just type in what you're called. But there's a strategy here.

Here's the rule: Your business name should be your actual, legal business name. Not keywords. Not 'Best Plumber in Denver.' Just your name.

Why? Because Google penalizes keyword stuffing. If you try to game it, Google catches it and you get ranked worse.

So if your business is 'Smith Plumbing', your name is 'Smith Plumbing'. Not 'Smith Plumbing - Denver's Best Plumber'.

Now, category. This is HUGE.

Your category is how Google understands what you do. It's the first signal Google uses to show you to the right people.

When someone searches 'plumber near me', Google shows businesses with the 'Plumber' category. If you're listed as 'Hardware Store', they won't show you.

So how do you choose the right category?

Think about what customers search for when looking for YOUR business. For a plumber, it's 'plumber', 'plumbing service', 'emergency plumber'. For a salon, it's 'hair salon', 'barber', 'hairstylist'.

Google gives you options. Usually 3-5 suggestions. Pick the one that matches what people search for.

Pro tip: You can have multiple categories. Add them all. If you're a salon that also does nails, add both 'Hair Salon' and 'Nail Salon'. This helps you show up for more searches.

Here's the step-by-step:

1. Go to your GMB dashboard
2. Click 'Edit profile'
3. Click on 'Business name'
4. Make sure it's your ACTUAL business name, not keywords
5. Click on 'Category'
6. Search for categories that match your business
7. Add primary category
8. Add additional categories (up to 10)
9. Save

That's it!

Quick example: You own 'Joe's Pizza'. Don't name it 'Joe's Pizza - Best Slice in Town'. Name it 'Joe's Pizza'. Then add categories like 'Pizza Restaurant', 'Italian Restaurant', 'Takeout', 'Delivery'.

This signals to Google exactly what you do, and you'll show up in more searches.

Let's move on to the next field."
